# Session note: vendor challenge articles

Use this pattern when a source article presents CTO challenges alongside solution marketing.

## Session takeaway
- Treat the article as a challenge inventory, not as authoritative guidance.
- Ignore sales pitches, product CTAs, and vendor-specific remedies.
- Reduce each challenge to the underlying CTO problem class.
- Compare the AI CTO response against the governing manual, not against the article’s recommended product.
- Keep the final output compact: challenge, expected CTO behavior, rating, and one-line reason.

## Practical filter
1. Extract challenge statements.
2. Drop pricing pages, CTA blocks, product comparisons, and marketing claims.
3. Normalize overlapping challenges into one class-level row.
4. Score whether the CTO response uses mitigation-first, phased options, ownership, escalation, and verification.

## Why this matters
Vendor blog posts often frame the problem in ways that smuggle in a solution. The stress test should measure CTO judgment, not the attractiveness of the pitch.
