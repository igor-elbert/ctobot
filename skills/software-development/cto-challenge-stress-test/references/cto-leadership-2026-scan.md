## Durable themes to carry into the checklist
- Customer value / business alignment remains primary.
- CTO leadership is broader than engineering: strategy, org design, communication, business acumen, operations, data, partnerships.
- Cross-functional influence is a core skill, not a nice-to-have.
- External credibility matters: boards, customers, partners, investors, media.
- Human-centered decision-making should be explicit, not implied.
- Resilience is both organizational and technical.
- Upskilling / workforce readiness is a shared leadership responsibility.
- ESG / sustainability belongs in the checklist when material to the business.

## Medium article breakdown
The Medium article grouped CTO capability into:
- Strategic leadership
- Organizational leadership and talent development
- Communication and influence
- Business acumen
- Innovation and transformation
- Technology vision and architecture
- Operational and execution excellence
- Data-driven decision making
- Collaboration across the C-suite
- Representing technology externally

These map well to a class-level CTO checklist and can be used as a source of sub-bullets when a user wants a broader leadership model.
